Getting Started

Boring Workout is a strength tracking app built around one principle: show you what's actually happening to your strength, not just log what you lifted.

When you first open the app your exercise database is pre-loaded with 70+ exercises across all major muscle groups and equipment types. No account, no sync, no subscription — all data stays on your device.

Recommended First Steps

  1. Go to Settings and enter your body weight. This unlocks relative strength rankings and the normalized Personal Strength Index.
  2. If you have a smart scale, enter body fat %, muscle mass %, and other body composition values. This unlocks additional strength-per-lean-mass metrics.
  3. Log your first workout using the Workout tab at the bottom.
  4. Log 3+ sessions of any exercise before the analytics section becomes meaningful. The app needs at least 3 data points to run trend analysis.
Body weight is the single most important setting. Without it, relative strength, allometric PSI, and tier comparisons cannot be computed.

Logging a Workout

Starting a Session

Tap the Workout tab. You'll see a Start Workout button if no session is active, or your Today's Plan if you have a routine with exercises assigned to today's day of the week. You can start a blank workout or start from a routine.

Adding Exercises

Tap Add Exercise to search the database. You can filter by:

  • Body region — Chest, Back, Shoulders, Arms, Legs, Core
  • Equipment — Barbell, Dumbbell, Cable, Machine, Bodyweight, Kettlebell
  • Compound vs isolation

Once an exercise is added, the app pre-fills sets with your most recent weight and reps for that exercise as targets.

Logging Sets

Each set row shows:

  • Target weight (grey) — from your last session for this exercise
  • Target reps (grey) — from last session or template
  • Actual weight and reps fields — tap to enter your numbers
  • Tap the checkmark to mark the set complete

The app tracks whether you hit, exceeded, or missed your target reps. This feeds into the progression feedback.

Supersets

Long-press an exercise row and tap Link as Superset to pair it with the next exercise. Paired exercises share a letter label (A, B, C…). Tap Unlink to separate them.

Finishing a Workout

Tap Finish Workout. You'll be asked to rate how the session felt:

RatingMeaningEffect
😴 TiredBody wasn't responding, forced the repsSession cost ×1.20
💪 NormalStandard sessionSession cost ×1.00
🔥 StrongEverything clicked, could have done moreSession cost ×0.85

After finishing, the app checks for new Personal Records and shows any PRs set during the session.

The feel rating takes 5 seconds and directly improves the session cost calculation, efficiency score, and coaching notes. Without it, every session is treated identically regardless of how you actually felt.

Workout Tab

The Workout tab serves three states:

Today's Plan

Exercises from your active routine scheduled for today's day of the week. Tap Start Today's Plan to pre-populate a workout with those exercises and your previous performance as targets.

Active Session

While a workout is in progress, this tab becomes your logging interface. All exercises, sets, and notes are shown here. The timer in the header shows your elapsed session time.

Rest Timer

Tap any completed set to start an optional rest timer. The timer runs in the background and sends a notification when rest is complete — so you can lock your phone between sets.

📋

Templates & Routines

Go to the Templates tab to create and manage routines.

Creating a Routine

  1. Tap New Routine
  2. Name it (e.g. "Push Day", "Full Body A")
  3. Add exercises
  4. For each exercise, set target sets and target reps
  5. Assign days of the week — this controls what appears in Today's Plan

Using a Routine

From the Workout tab, tap Start Today's Plan to auto-populate a workout from your routine. You can also tap Start Full Routine to start all exercises regardless of day assignment.

Swapping Exercises

During an active workout started from a routine, you can swap any exercise for a different one while keeping the same set/rep structure. Long-press the exercise row and tap Swap Exercise.

Strength Score

Four ways to assess your strength, switchable via a tab picker. The default tab is Overall.

Overall — Composite Strength Score (CSS)

The CSS is a single 0–100 score that answers: "How well am I doing at getting stronger right now?" It combines every metric in the app into three pillars:

35%
Level
Current e1RM average as % of your personal best. Drops after deloads, rises as you approach PRs.
40%
Momentum
How fast you're improving right now. OLS %/week trend. 0%/wk = 50pts, +2%/wk = 100pts.
25%
Process
Training quality — INOL (40%) + Efficiency (40%) + Rep Decay (20%).

Grade Scale

D50–59Below par
F< 50Declining

The coaching insight below the grade badge identifies the weakest pillar and gives one specific, actionable recommendation. The Score History chart shows CSS per session over time, colour-coded by grade.

Fiber Index — Personal Strength Index (PSI)

PSI converts every exercise you do into a universal currency: estimated muscle fiber units. It uses published EMG activation data and anatomical PCSA values (physiological cross-sectional area — a proxy for fiber count) to weight each exercise by how much real muscle tissue it recruits at what intensity.

PSI Formula
PSI_raw = Σ_exercises Σ_sets ( w ÷ ref_e1RM )^1.8 × reps × Σ_muscles ( EMG% × PCSA ) PSI_norm = PSI_raw ÷ bodyweight^0.67

You can view PSI in four sub-modes:

ModeFormulaWhat it tells you
RawPSI_rawTotal fiber load units. Best for tracking your own trend.
÷ Body WeightPSI ÷ BW^0.67Allometric scaling — removes size advantage. Comparable across your timeline as weight changes.
÷ Lean MassPSI ÷ LeanMass^0.67Output per unit of lean tissue. Requires body fat % in Settings.
÷ Muscle MassPSI ÷ MuscleMass^0.67Output per unit of skeletal muscle. Requires muscle mass % in Settings.
A rising PSI means more total muscle work over time. A flat PSI while e1RM is rising means you're getting more efficient — the same fiber work is producing more strength output.

Body Comp

Shows body composition data from your smart scale alongside three derived ratios that track how efficiently your lean tissue is being trained:

  • PSI ÷ Lean Mass^0.67 — fiber output per unit of lean tissue. Rising = improving neural efficiency or muscle quality.
  • PSI ÷ Muscle Mass^0.67 — how hard your skeletal muscle is actually working per session.
  • Fiber Load ÷ Body Fat% — output relative to fat mass. Rises when you lose fat while maintaining strength, or gain strength while keeping fat stable.

Requires body fat % and/or muscle mass % entered in Settings → Body Composition.

Progress Tracker

Two auto-generated lists from your recent training:

Getting Stronger

Exercises with a positive trend over the last 6 weeks. Shows absolute gain — most recent e1RM minus oldest in the window.

Needs Attention

Exercises where the OLS slope is below 0.5 kg/week over 4+ weeks with 3+ sessions. These are genuinely stalled — not just slow.

Common fixes: Add 1.25–2.5 kg next session · Change rep range (3×5 → 4×8) · Take a planned deload · Check sleep and protein intake.

Personal Records

Every exercise's all-time best estimated 1RM, grouped by body region and sorted strongest first. PRs are detected automatically when you finish a workout — if a set's Epley e1RM exceeds the stored PR for that exercise, the new set becomes the record. Each card shows the actual weight × reps, the date, and the estimated 1RM.

Getting the Most Out of the App

Watch your Composite Strength Score (CSS) as your primary weekly signal

It aggregates every metric. If it's rising, you're doing the right things regardless of which individual metric looks off. If it's falling despite consistent training, the coaching insight will identify exactly which pillar is dragging it down.

Log the feel rating every session

It takes 5 seconds and directly improves the session cost calculation, efficiency score, and coaching notes. Without it, every session is treated identically regardless of how you actually felt.

Log at least 3 sessions before trusting trends

Two sessions is not enough for a meaningful regression. The trend line, plateau detection, and efficiency score all need at least 3 data points per exercise.

Enter your body weight in Settings

Almost all normalized metrics require it. If your weight changes significantly, update it — the PSI trend will shift but that's correct.

Use the Efficiency Score as your primary long-term signal

A rising efficiency score means you're getting more strength adaptation per unit of fatigue produced — the best possible outcome in strength training.

Watch the INOL on your main lifts

If INOL is consistently below 0.4 (Low zone) for a stalled exercise, you're not providing enough stimulus. If it's above 2.0 (Overreaching) and you're not recovering, pull back.

Trust the stall flag

The app flags an exercise as stalled only after 3+ sessions in 4 weeks with slope below 0.5 kg/week. By that point, continuing to do the same thing will not produce different results.

Update body composition data regularly

If you're running a cut or bulk, your PSI ÷ lean mass and PSI ÷ muscle mass metrics will tell you whether strength is moving in line with body composition changes.

All data is stored locally on your device. Nothing is sent to any server.
