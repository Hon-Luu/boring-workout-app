2 The Realistic High-Performing Consumer Version

After compressing gold-standard approaches through the constraints of a consumer mobile app — minimal friction, imperfect data, normal compliance — this is what a high-performing version looks like. These are achievable without hardware, without clinical data, and with normal user behavior.

What Gets Built

  • One readiness question, not zero. Pre-workout: "How ready do you feel? 1–5." Displayed on the Home screen as a prominent tap, not buried. This single input, captured 70%+ of the time, would unlock the readinessBefore modifier and the mindBodyAlignment insight. Currently: 0/18. One UX change.
  • RPE on the last set only. Not every set — the last one. "How hard was that? 1–10." After the user already feels the effort, one tap. This populates 8 of 14 EmergentInsight prerequisites. Currently: 0/149.
  • Weighted recent history for coaching. Weight suggestion uses the last 3 sessions average, not just the last session. Anomalous sessions (zero-rep, floor-weight) receive less weight. One failed session does not contaminate the target pre-fill.
  • Deload based on rolling 2-week trend, not single session. Current deload trigger (2 consecutive sessions under 65%) is too hair-trigger. Validated deload criteria: ≥3 sessions showing performance decline over ≥2 weeks (Zourdos 2016).
  • Trainer tab in navigation. The entire plan generation feature exists and is unreachable. Fix the navigation. This is a one-day engineering change with zero scientific dependencies.
  • Gap return message at 3 days, not 7. Current HONHabitEngine fires returnAfterLapse only at ≥7 days. Research shows the churn risk window begins at day 3 (Fogg 2020 — habits break at disruption points, recovery must be immediate).
  • Seed WorkoutPlanEngine. Plans should not change on every render. One seed, stable plan for the day. Change on next app open or manual refresh.
  • Cap visualizations to 3 per view. Hick's Law: decision time increases logarithmically with options. 55 visualizations is an analytics showcase, not a fitness tool.

What Gets Removed or Hidden

  • Fatigue-adjusted e1RM chart. Requires RPE data. 0/149 sets have RPE. The chart's second series is identical to the first series. Hidden until RPE capture rate exceeds 20%.
  • INOL zones in user-facing views. INOL (Intensity + Number of Lifts) is a powerlifting programming metric. Consumer users don't know what it means and can't act on it. Keep in engine, remove from UI.
  • Fiber load / primary muscle contributor grid. Scientifically interesting, operationally meaningless for users logging with phone in hand. Keep as a "for enthusiasts" deep-dive, not front-surface analytics.
  • EmergentInsights carousel when <4 are populated. A carousel rotating through 10 placeholder cards and 4 real ones signals "this app doesn't know you yet" — which is the opposite of the personalization promise. Show 1 specific insight or nothing.
  • 12 of 14 EmergentInsights behind HealthKit wall. If HealthKit isn't connected, surface one card: "Connect Apple Health to unlock 12 additional insights." Not a carousel of locked tiles.
  • PCSA-weighted labels in user-facing views. The Fiber Load card shows "raw a.u." and "allometric PSI" values to users who cannot interpret them. These are engineer-facing metrics presented as user value. Remove unit labels or add a single-sentence explanation.

3 Highest-Risk Assumptions — With Expert Disagreements

These are not hypothetical risks. Each is grounded in the codebase, the data map, or documented behavior patterns. Expert disagreements are shown explicitly — the panel does not agree on severity.

A1 — The Readiness Score Is Materially Incomplete Without HealthKit

The engine has 10+ modifiers. Sleep hours (+5/+2/−5/−10), resting HR (+3/−3/−6), steps (0.3–0.85 multiplier), and readinessBefore trend (±4–5) are all inactive when HealthKit is disconnected. Of 18 sessions, HealthKit data has never been connected. The score the user sees is produced by, at most, 4 of 10+ modifiers — daysSinceLast, consecutiveActive, recentFreq, volumeSpike, and RPE (also null). A user with 5 hours of sleep and elevated HR gets the same score as a fully rested user.

Furthermore: the baseline itself is dynamic — 65 + min(15, sessions_last30d) — but the CLAUDE.md documentation states the baseline as a fixed "68." This is not true. With 18 sessions in 30 days, the baseline is 80, not 68. The worked example in the audit approach document is wrong by 11 points.

AI Safety Reviewer

This is not a minor gap. A readiness score that ignores sleep is not a readiness score — it's a training frequency score dressed up as personalized wellness. If the score is presented as "readiness" and it ignores the most evidence-supported predictor of athletic performance (sleep), the product is making a claim it cannot support. Severity: P0.

Consumer App Product Lead

Most fitness apps operate without HRV or sleep data. The score still provides useful directional signal — "you trained 5 days in a row" is real information even without sleep data. The fix is labeling: if HealthKit is disconnected, show "Training Readiness" (what it actually measures) not "Readiness" (what it implies).

Sports Scientist

The dynamic baseline finding is more concerning than the HealthKit gap. If the score can range from 65 (no sessions) to 80 (fully active) as a baseline alone, and this is undocumented, the modifiers are being applied against an opaque denominator. Users cannot understand why their score changed.

A2 — The Coaching Narrative Will Repeat Within 4–6 Weeks for Active Users

The phrase bank rotation uses a 10-phrase window per exercise per category. With a user logging the same exercise 2–3 times per week, each phrase category is hit approximately every 3–5 sessions. The 10-phrase window means repetition at exactly session 10 for the most-used exercise/category combination. At 18 sessions, any exercise logged ≥5 times has almost certainly produced a repeated coaching line.

When coaching repeats, the personalization illusion breaks. Users who notice repetition categorize the coaching as "copy-paste" regardless of whether it was technically correct. This is the transition from "the app knows me" to "the app is a template." Once that transition occurs, it rarely reverses.

Elite Strength Coach

This is the most serious long-term risk in the product. Real coaching becomes repetitive too — but real coaches build a relationship that makes repetition acceptable. This app has no relationship layer. When the user notices the same phrase in week 6 that appeared in week 2, there is no relationship to absorb the disappointment. The coaching must either have genuine novelty or the relationship must be built elsewhere.

Behavioral Psychologist

Mild disagreement on severity. Research on habit formation shows that after automaticity (roughly 66 sessions — Lally 2010), users no longer need motivational coaching — they need confirmation. Repetitive confirmation is acceptable. The problem is that 18 sessions is still in the habit-building window, where novelty matters most.

A3 — 12 of 14 EmergentInsights Are Placeholders for This User

Of 14 EmergentInsight types, a systematic check against the data map:

  • Mind-Body Alignment: needs HRV ≥65ms → HealthKit absent → proxy only
  • True Adaptation State: needs velocity >0.3 kg/wk → velocity 0/149 → disabled
  • Program Calibration: needs RPE ≥7.5 → 0/149 RPE → disabled
  • Net Recovery Capacity: needs sleep hours → HealthKit absent → disabled
  • PR Attempt Quality: needs velocity data → 0/149 → disabled
  • Cardio Fatigue Pattern: needs RPE within 48h → RPE null → disabled
  • Body Weight × Strength: needs ≥4 weight entries over ≥2 months → unclear
  • Aerobic Capacity: needs VO2 Max (HealthKit) → absent → disabled
  • Rest Day Activity: needs step data (HealthKit) → absent → disabled
  • Sleep & Peak Performance: needs ≥3 PR sessions → possibly active
  • Strongest Day: needs ≥15 sessions, ≥3 same weekday → possibly active at 18 sessions
  • Feel Archetype: needs ≥6 sessions + feel → 15/18 feel → likely active
  • Load Tolerance Profile: needs ≥5 sessions + feel + duration >60m → possibly active
  • Imbalance Trajectory: needs ≥2 category analytics → likely active

Result: approximately 3–4 of 14 insights are live. The carousel shows 10–11 placeholders rotating with 3–4 real insights. The feature's visual presence exceeds its data-backed substance by a factor of 3–4×.

UX Researcher

This is a trust destruction mechanism. When users see a card that says "Connect Apple Health to unlock this insight" five times in the same carousel, the net message is "this app doesn't work for me." The feature should be hidden entirely until 8+ insights can be populated, then introduced as a meaningful unlock event.

Consumer App Product Lead

Placeholders can be intentional — they show aspiration and create a pull toward HealthKit connection. This only works if the placeholders are specific ("Connect Health to see how your sleep affects your deadlift performance") not generic ("Not enough data"). Check the actual placeholder text quality before making a verdict.

Data Scientist

Three active insights from 14 is a 21% utilization rate for the feature's primary screen real estate. From a product metrics standpoint, this is a failing feature that is being surfaced at full visual weight. The feature needs a data gate before it appears, not a post-loading placeholder replacement.

A4 — The Allometric Scaling and PCSA Inputs Are Systematically Wrong

The muscleMassPercent field stores 79.85 (likely lean body mass % — body weight minus fat). The UI labels it "Muscle Mass %" with a valid range of 10–65%. True skeletal muscle mass for an adult male is 30–50% of body weight. Lean body mass is 70–85%. The stored value is lean body mass, labeled as muscle mass. Every PCSA-dependent calculation (fiber load, allometric PSI, primary muscle contributor grid) uses this input. The values shown are wrong by a systematic factor, calculated from a mislabeled input against the wrong reference range.

Engineering Lead note: The PCSA computation uses population-mean fiber PCSA × the user's muscleMassPercent as a scaling factor. With 79.85 entered where the formula expects 30–50 (skeletal muscle), the PSI values displayed are inflated by roughly 1.5–2×. This doesn't affect progression tracking (relative values are still meaningful) but makes the absolute PSI numbers uninterpretable and scientifically invalid.

A5 — The Deload and Plateau Detection Have Insufficient Statistical Power at 18 Sessions

Plateau detection requires ≥3 sessions over ≥4 weeks with OLS slope <0.5 kg/wk. With 18 sessions across 19 exercises, most exercises have ≤3 sessions total. The OLS slope from 2–3 points has enormous variance. A "plateau" detected from 3 sessions may reverse on the 4th. A "momentum" classification from 3 sessions may be noise.

The deload trigger (2 consecutive sessions <65% of target completion) is particularly unstable at low session counts. One zero-rep session followed by a cautious second session triggers the deload flag without meaningful evidence of systematic overtraining.

Data Scientist

With 3 data points, OLS has 1 degree of freedom. The confidence interval on a slope from 3 points is so wide that it is statistically indistinguishable from zero. Displaying a "plateau" classification from 3 sessions is presenting noise as signal. The minimum threshold for reliable OLS trend detection in this context is 6–8 sessions of the same exercise.

Sports Scientist

Practical objection: waiting for 6–8 sessions of the same exercise before showing trend data means waiting 6–12 weeks for most users. That's too long to be actionable. A 3-session trend with explicit uncertainty labeling is better than no trend at all. The fix is confidence communication, not the threshold.

6 The 20% of Features Responsible for 80% of User Outcomes

If the app shipped with only these features, it would retain more users at 90 days than the full current feature set. These are the features that directly produce the outcomes users actually want: progressive overload, habit formation, and the belief that the app works.

The Core Six

  • Progressive overload tracking. The fundamental driver of strength gains. The app logs weight and reps, computes e1RM, and compares to prior sessions. This is the entire scientific basis of the product. Every other feature derives value from whether this is correct.
  • Post-set coaching narrative. The primary emotional experience. The moment after a set is the highest-engagement point in the session. A specific, accurate coaching response here is worth more than 10 additional analytics charts. Quality matters more than sophistication.
  • PR detection and acknowledgment. The single highest-stakes reward signal in strength training. A personal record is an identity event, not a data point. The current implementation (banner + guard prevBest > 0) is correct in principle. Display quality and emotional weight of the PR moment are the execution questions.
  • Gap return framing. The make-or-break retention moment. A user who returns after 5 days away and feels welcomed comes back again. A user who feels behind, punished, or scolded does not. This single UX moment has a larger effect on 90-day retention than any analytics feature.
  • Feel rating at session end. The only subjective enrichment signal that is being captured at scale (83%). It feeds coaching coherence, EmergentInsights, and readiness proxying. More important than any objective metric because it's available.
  • Pre-filled weight/rep target from prior session. The single most valuable UX affordance for logging speed. Users who see their prior performance auto-filled log faster and with less friction. The quality of this pre-fill (what happens after an anomalous session) is directly correlated with user trust in the app's intelligence.

The Two That Unlock the Tier Above

  • readinessBefore capture. One UX fix. This single field, if captured consistently, enables: ReadinessEngine mindBodyAlignment modifier, readinessBefore trend modifier (±4–5 points), and the mindBodyAlignment EmergentInsight. It also differentiates this app from every competitor that treats readiness as a post-hoc calculation. Capture requires a non-dismissible pre-workout prompt — identical to the FeelSelectorSheet approach already implemented for feel.
  • RPE on last set. One additional tap per exercise. This enables: fatigue-adjusted e1RM (the differentiated analytics feature), ProgramCalibration insight, CardioFatigue insight, RPE trend in ExerciseInsightDetailView. Currently 0/149. A single design change — move the RPE tap to immediately follow the "Complete Set" action — would change this.
The 80/20 in plain English: Log the workout. Show accurate progress. Say something specific after each set. Welcome users back without guilt. These four things, done well, are the product. Everything else is optionality that only advanced users will discover and value.

  • Discovery: Trainer tab exists but isn't in nav. "Why is this here and not reachable?"
  • HealthKit refused or not connected → Health tiles empty → entire health analytics layer is dead weight
  • Strong is faster to log. Speed matters to this cohort more than depth.

High gap frequency · 3–10 day gaps common · Guilt-prone on return · Training is aspirational

The Gap Return Sequence

  • Day 4 of gap: readiness −4 (daysSinceLast). HONHabitEngine silent. No message.
  • Day 7 of gap: HONHabitEngine fires returnAfterLapse. This is the first contact since the gap began.
  • Days 4–6 are the critical window. The app is silent during the period when the user most needs a non-judgmental nudge.
  • Return: readiness shows a lower score than on their last workout day. They read this as "the app knows I've been bad."

The Guilt Loop

  • User returns after 8 days. HONHabitEngine message fires. If message references days away: abandonment risk.
  • No "recovery plan" offered. Just: here's the current readiness score and a blank workout.
  • Progress tab shows gap in timeline. Visual punishment even if text is neutral.
  • No "I was sick / traveling / injured" flag. Gap = default = motivational failure in app's implicit framing.

What They Actually Need

  • Day 3–4 of gap: proactive non-judgmental message. "Good day to come back whenever you're ready."
  • Return framing: forward, not backward. "Your muscles have had 8 days to recover. Your readiness reflects recovery, not loss."
  • Easy re-entry: one tap from Home to a "comeback session" — lower volume, familiar exercises.
  • No streak count visible on return. Show next milestone instead of broken streak.

Abandonment Trigger

  • Any language during gap return that references the absence negatively → permanent churn for this cohort
  • The brand promise ("never punish gaps") must survive every edge case in HONHabitEngine and ReadinessEngine framing
  • Coaching after return session: if it praises the return with excessive enthusiasm, feels fake. Needs to be dry and specific.

Cohort 4: Time-Constrained Users (10 users)

Logging Speed Test

  • Time from app open to first set logged: target ≤15 seconds. Actual: likely 20–30 seconds with exercise selection and navigation.
  • Per-set logging: weight → reps → complete. 3 taps minimum. Coaching text after each set adds ~3 seconds of read time even if skipped.
  • Feel rating at session end: one additional step before closing. They skip it. 17% of sessions already missing this.

What They Use

  • The pre-filled weight from prior session is their most valuable feature — fastest path to logging
  • They do not open Progress, Insights, or StrengthLab tabs
  • Coaching text is scrolled past without reading
  • The app is purely a logbook for this cohort

Where Friction Builds

  • WeightSuggestionSheet appearing on exercise open — interrupts their flow
  • Any modal or sheet that requires dismissal before logging begins
  • The readiness prompt (if it appears) is dismissed immediately
  • Navigation to a new exercise from the exercise library is slower than typing a name

Retention Mechanic That Works

  • The app remembers their routine → they don't have to recreate it → fastest path to logging
  • Pre-filled targets mean they log in 60% of the taps a fresh session would require
  • PR banner is a genuine moment even for this cohort — it stops them mid-scroll
  • This cohort stays longer than expected because the app is a fast logbook with memory

The Injury Scenario

  • User sprains wrist in week 4. Cannot bench press, push movements impossible.
  • App has no "injury" flag, no exercise substitution, no lower-body-only template.
  • Readiness score continues to show training gap penalty. The score frames injury as laziness.
  • After 2–3 weeks of silence, user feels too far behind to return. The gap feels permanent.

The App's Response

  • Days 1–6 of injury: silence
  • Day 7+: HONHabitEngine returnAfterLapse fires
  • Return message: assumes motivational gap, not injury
  • No modification to readiness framing: score shows "training gap" penalty, not "recovery period"

What Would Keep Them

  • An "I'm injured / resting" mode — pauses streak, disables gap penalty, surfaces accessible exercises
  • Exercise substitution: "Can't bench? Try cable fly, chest press machine, or push-up variations"
  • Readiness framing during injury: "Your upper body is resting. Lower body readiness: strong."
  • Return protocol: reduced-volume comeback session pre-loaded

Stakes

  • Injuries happen to ~60% of recreational lifters per year (Aasa 2017). This is not an edge case.
  • If the app has no response to injury, it loses the user at the exact moment they most need support.
  • This is a feature gap with high churn impact and moderate implementation complexity.

The Simplified Product in One Paragraph

Fix the data entry errors first (zero-rep, dumbbell convention, muscleMassPercent label). Add readinessBefore as a non-dismissible pre-workout prompt. Move RPE to post-last-set. Seed the plan engine. Put the Trainer tab in navigation. Fire the gap return message at 3 days. Show the OverloadScoreboard as the hero card in Progress. Cap each view to 3 visible charts with progressive disclosure. Expand the phrase bank to 30+ entries per category with session-specific data references. Gate the EmergentInsights carousel to populated insights only. The product that does these 10 things retains more users, builds more trust, and delivers more real-world outcome improvement than the product that also has INOL zones, allometric PSI, rep decay metrics, and 55 visualizations.
