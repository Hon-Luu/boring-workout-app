CON-01
Readiness "Don't hold back" vs Mind-Body Alignment "Athletes get hurt in this state"
Home · Readiness coaching note Progress · Emerging Insights
›
Message A
Home tab → ReadinessCoachCard → ReadinessEngine.narrative() score ≥ 80
Trigger: good training frequency + adequate sleep + low ATL → score climbs above 80
"You've been recovering well and the momentum is there. Don't hold back today — this is the kind of day you make progress on."
Message B
Progress tab → Emerging Insights → MindBodyAlignment = OVERCONFIDENT
Trigger: low HRV (physiology says fatigued) + feel rating "Strong" — feel outpaces biology
"Feel is high but physiology says otherwise. High failure risk on PR attempts. Athletes get hurt in this state."
Root cause
ReadinessEngine does not read HRV. It scores from training frequency, sleep, RHR, and volume ratio. MindBodyAlignment uses HRV × feel. The two engines can reach opposite conclusions on the same day from non-overlapping inputs. No bridge between them.
Specific trigger scenario
User has been training consistently (freq=4, good sleep this week, low ATL) → Readiness lands ~82. Today HRV is 48 ms (suppressed from illness or life stress) but subjective feel is "Strong" → OVERCONFIDENT state fires in Progress. Both surfaces visible before and after workout.
Proposed Fix
When MindBodyAlignment == .overconfident AND ReadinessEngine score ≥ 80 AND HRV is available: override the Readiness coaching note to: "Recovery data looks solid but your HRV is below baseline. Aim for 80% effort today — your feel is running ahead of your physiology." Requires passing mindBodyState into ReadinessEngine.narrative().

PRAttemptQuality fix is softer language when velocity is positive: change FOOL'S GOLD implication to include acknowledgment that "your last session was strong — confirm the progression next session after a rest day." Alternative: add a small disclaimer to the "Progress Weight" phrase bank entries when TSB is negative, e.g., "Earned the increase — take a rest day first, then go up."

CON-05
Readiness "Get your work in, keep the streak alive" vs Net Recovery "Deload is not optional — it's structural"
Home · ReadinessCoachCard (score 65–79) Progress · Emerging Insights · Net Recovery Capacity
›
Message A — Readiness coaching (score 65–79)
Home tab → ReadinessEngine.narrative() → score 65–79 band
Trigger: reasonable training frequency + sleep 6–7h (neutral zone, no penalty) → score in mid-range
"Body feels balanced and you're in a good rhythm. Get your work in, stay focused, keep the streak alive."
Message B — Net Recovery Capacity
Progress → EmergentInsightEngine → NetRecoveryCapacity = .compoundedDeficit
Trigger: sleep < 7h AND acute training load > 55/100 — both conditions simultaneously active
"Sleep debt and training debt simultaneously active. Recovery capacity is critically low. A deload is not optional — it's structural."
Root cause
ReadinessEngine's sleep penalty requires sleep below 6h to fire (-5 pts). At 6.0–6.9h it applies no adjustment, leaving score neutral. NetRecoveryCapacity uses 7h as its threshold. A user sleeping 6.5h AND training heavily fails the Progress check but passes the Readiness check. Result: "everything fine, train on" vs "critical deload required."
Specific trigger scenario
User averages 6.5h sleep this week, trains 4× (ATL = 62). Readiness: sleep 6.5h = no penalty, freq 4 = +5, daysSinceLast=1 = +8 → ~81... actually might go high. More likely: sleep 6.2h (no penalty), daysSinceLast=3 (-4) → ~69. COMPOUNDED DEFICIT fires in Progress. Readiness says "good rhythm."
Proposed Fix
Lower ReadinessEngine's sleep threshold to 7.0h (matching NetRecovery standard): apply a small penalty (-2 pts) for sleep 6.0–6.99h instead of treating it as neutral. This aligns the two systems' view of "adequate sleep." Also: when NetRecovery fires COMPOUNDED DEFICIT, the Readiness coaching note should echo the deload recommendation rather than contradicting it.

Phrase bank key resolves to: "Try [~10% heavier] for 3–5 reps next session — the nervous system sometimes needs a heavier signal to break a plateau." (assembled from whats_next bank)
Message B — Mind-Body Alignment
Progress → EmergentInsightEngine → MindBodyAlignment = .overconfident
Trigger: low HRV + feel rating "Strong" — physiology suppressed, subjective state elevated
"Feel is high but physiology says otherwise. High failure risk on PR attempts. Athletes get hurt in this state."
Root cause
The neural overload technique (go heavier to break a stall) is functionally a PR attempt strategy. It requires neurological headroom. Specific trigger scenario

In WorkoutNarrativeEngine, when the whatsNextKey == "Stuck — Neural Overload", add a conditional: if MindBodyAlignment == .overconfident (accessible via analyticsResult or by passing the computed state), downgrade recommendation to "Stuck — Rest & Microload" and note: "HRV suggests today isn't the day for a heavy stimulus. Add a recovery session first, then try the heavier signal."
