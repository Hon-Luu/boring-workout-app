Quick Start Guide

H.O.N

Health OS — from first workout to full insight in minutes

1. First-time setup (3 minutes)

Do this once. It unlocks your strength tier, relative-strength ratios, and personalised readiness scores.

STEP 1

Enter your body weight

Settings → Strength Profile → Body Weight (kg). This is required for relative strength ratios, PSI scores, and tier classification.

Without body weight, tiers default to Intermediate and relative-strength charts stay hidden.
STEP 2

Grant HealthKit access

Settings → Health OS. Allows HRV, resting HR, VO₂ max, and step count to feed your readiness score and "What Influences Me" panel.

The app works without HealthKit — readiness runs on workout log data alone.
STEP 3

Build a routine (optional)

Workout tab → Templates → + — create a Push/Pull/Legs or any split. Assign exercises and days so your home screen shows today's plan automatically.

You can log freely without a routine too — just tap "Start Workout" from the home screen.

3. Log your first strength workout

The fastest path from screen-on to sets logged.

  1. Tap "Start Workout" on the Home screen Or go to the Workout tab and tap the start button. A timer begins automatically.
  2. Add an exercise Tap "Add Exercise" → search by name or browse by muscle group. Recently used exercises appear at the top of the list.
  3. Log your sets Tap a set row to enter weight and reps. Previous session's data pre-fills as a target. Tap the checkmark to complete each set.
  4. Optional: Add a drop set After completing a set, tap the drop-set icon. Enter the drop weight and reps. The app keeps both in its e1RM calculation and will warn you if the drop weight exceeds the main set weight.
  5. Optional: Mark "to failure" or add RPE Long-press or tap the extras icon on any set for failure flag and RPE (6–10). These feed the training quality (Process) score.
  6. Rate the session feel When you finish, you'll be asked Tired / Normal / Strong. Honest answers improve coaching note accuracy over time.
  7. Tap "Finish Workout" Analytics rebuild in the background. Your e1RM trend, PSI, and readiness score update automatically.
Pro tip: After 3 sessions on any exercise, the app unlocks its e1RM trend chart, INOL score, rep decay, and efficiency label. After 10 total sessions, readiness confidence rises to "High."
New in Version 2
  • Log any activity — tap "Log Activity" on the Home screen to record yoga, cycling, hiking, and more.
  • PR detection is automatic — a gold banner appears when you hit a new personal record during a workout.
  • Insight Levels — in Settings → Workout, choose Essential (simple), Standard, or Expert to control how much analytics you see.

4. Log a circuit / cardio session

  1. Build a circuit Workout tab → "Start a Circuit" → tap + to create. Name it, choose AMRAP (max rounds in time) or EMOM (one exercise per minute), set duration, pick exercises and target reps. Optionally set a weight for weighted circuits.
  2. Schedule it (optional) In the circuit builder, toggle the days you want it to appear on your Home screen automatically.
  3. Start the session Tap the circuit → Start. A 3-2-1 countdown launches. EMOM: the app calls out each exercise by voice and counts down the minute. AMRAP: a global countdown ring runs while you track reps per exercise.
  4. Adjust reps in real time Use the +/− buttons for each exercise. EMOM auto-saves your rep count when the minute ends — you don't have to tap anything between minutes.
  5. Session ends automatically (or tap "End Session") A summary shows rounds completed, total reps, duration, and per-exercise breakdowns. If your circuit uses weights, total volume (kg·reps) is shown too.
Background mode: The EMOM countdown voice and timer continue when the phone is locked or the app is backgrounded — background audio is fully enabled.

5. Reading your Progress tab

Composite Strength Score (CSS)

  • Graded S → F (0–100 scale)
  • Three pillars: Level (35%), Momentum (40%), Process (25%)
  • Requires data from Push, Pull, and Legs to reach Elite — missing categories cap the tier
  • Tap the score card for a full breakdown

e1RM Trend Charts

  • One chart per exercise with ≥ 3 sessions
  • Raw line + 5-session rolling average
  • Fatigue-adjusted line recovers "rested" capacity
  • Slope shown as kg/week (6-week window)

Readiness Score (Home)

  • 0–100 score combining days since last workout, session frequency, and volume trend
  • Counts both strength AND cardio sessions
  • Coach note updates immediately after each session

Cardio Analytics

  • Session count vs. last week, total rounds, avg reps/min
  • Per-exercise rep progression sparklines (≥ 3 sessions)
  • 8 cross-domain insights after enough data builds

7. Five habits that maximise the app

✓ Log every set honestly

The e1RM engine needs real rep counts. Logging "3 sets of 5" when you actually did 4, 3, 3 hides your fatigue curve and skews INOL.

✓ Rate your feel every session

The Tired/Normal/Strong feel rating multiplies your session cost by 0.85×, 1.0×, or 1.15×. Honest ratings let the app detect overtraining signals accurately.

✓ Log circuits AND strength

Cardio sessions count toward your streak, readiness, and 8 cross-domain insights. A pure cardio week no longer looks like zero training.

✓ Keep body weight current

Relative strength (e1RM ÷ BW) and PSI normalization use the most recently entered body weight. Update it monthly for accurate tier tracking.

✓ Cover all three PPL categories

The Composite Strength Score can only reach Advanced with 2 of 3 categories covered, and Elite only with all 3. Push, Pull, and Legs are each required.
